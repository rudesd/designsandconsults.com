Local SEO

HOW TO OWN PAGE 1 ON GOOGLE IN YOUR SERVICE AREA

By Rudy — RJM Designs & Consultations ◆ February 28, 2025 ◆ 6 min read
Let's be honest — most contractors have no idea how Google decides who shows up first when someone searches for their trade in their city. They just know they're not showing up, and they're not sure why.

The good news is local SEO for contractors is actually one of the more predictable parts of digital marketing. Unlike broad SEO — which can take years and require massive content budgets — local SEO rewards consistency, completeness, and reputation. Three things every contractor can control.

Here's exactly what moves the needle.
01

Understand the Two Places You Can Rank

When someone searches 'HVAC contractor in [city],' Google shows two types of results: the local map pack (three businesses shown with a map) and the organic blue links below it.

The map pack is driven primarily by your Google Business Profile. The organic results are driven by your website. You want to show up in both — but if you're starting from scratch, focus on the map pack first. It's faster to influence, it's what most people click, and it shows your star rating and reviews right in the results.

02

Optimize Your Google Business Profile From Top to Bottom

Google uses your GBP to decide where you rank in the map pack. The more complete and active your profile, the higher you rank. It really is that direct.

Start with your business category — make sure your primary category is the most specific option that describes your main service. Then fill out every single section: services, service area, hours, business description (use your primary keyword in the first sentence), photos, and Q&A. A profile that's 100% complete will outrank one that's 60% complete almost every time.

After the initial setup, activity matters. Post weekly updates. Respond to every review within 48 hours. Add new photos regularly. Google rewards active profiles with better rankings.

03

Build Local Citations Consistently

A citation is any online mention of your business name, address, and phone number. Google uses citations to verify that your business is legitimate and to understand your service area.

The most important thing about citations isn't quantity — it's consistency. Your business name, address, and phone number need to be identical across every directory. Even small differences ('St.' vs 'Street,' a slightly different phone format) can confuse Google's algorithm and suppress your rankings.

Start with the big ones: Yelp, Angi, HomeAdvisor, Facebook, Apple Maps, Bing Places, and your local Chamber of Commerce directory. Make sure they all match exactly. Then work your way through industry-specific directories for your trade.

04

Get More Reviews — Consistently, Not Just Once

Reviews are one of the top ranking factors for the Google map pack. Not just the number of reviews, but the recency and the rate at which you're getting them. A business that consistently gets 3–4 new reviews every month will outrank a business that got 50 reviews two years ago and then stopped.

This is why a review generation system matters so much. You can't just ask for reviews when you remember to — it has to be baked into your post-job process every single time.

05

Optimize Your Website for Local Keywords

Your website needs to tell Google what you do and where you do it — explicitly. Not just implied. The title tag on your homepage should include your trade and your city. Your H1 heading should include your primary service. Your page content should naturally mention your service area multiple times.

If you serve multiple cities, consider creating a dedicated page for each one. A page titled 'Plumbing Services in [City]' with 400–600 words of relevant content can rank for that city specifically, giving you multiple entry points in the search results.

This isn't about keyword stuffing — it's about being clear. Write for your customer first, and make sure Google can understand what you do and who you serve.

06

Be Patient — But Start Today

Local SEO results don't happen overnight. A fully optimized GBP can start showing movement in 4–8 weeks. A well-optimized website with strong citations and consistent reviews can reach page 1 in 3–6 months for most local markets.

The contractors who dominate their local search results aren't there by accident. They started working on this 6 months ago. The best time to start is right now — the second best time is never.
